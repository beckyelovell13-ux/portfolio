Getting a lockdown doggo

December 2021
Animals

Anyone who knows me personally, knows that I have always been obsessed (and I mean obsessed) with dogs! I had many books as a child that went into depth about every dog breed that exists, and I would read it for hours upon hours, getting to know the various traits, types & the history of each breed. I still, today, would say that aside from UI/UX, dog breeds is the subject I pride myself on knowing the most about.

I've always loved most animals, mainly due to their innocent nature and lifestyle, the way they only seem to take what they need, unlike the greed I see a lot from human beings. But mostly the loyalty and companionship dogs bring has always charmed me.

I had a chocolate labarador growing up named Todd, he was my best friend and I felt he gave me a purpose in life, a reason to get outside and go on adventures. As I got older, I knew that getting a dog of my own was a huge goal of mine, I needed that sense of purpose and a reason to get outside into the fresh air.

I was renting with my partner, but we weren't allowed pets at the property, so I knew I had to wait until we'd saved enough money to purchase our first house. It took a good 5 years until we were in the position to be able to do this, and by then I had manifested the idea of having a fluffy companion so much that it was starting to become unhealthy!

Even though, as I mentioned earlier, I have a pretty good source of knowledge in my brain when it comes to dog breeds... I obviously had a very long time to research the ins and outs of every one to help me make the almost impossible decision of which breed to choose!

Eventually after a lot of back and fourth in my mind, I decided that a Miniature Goldendoodle would be the perfect breed for us! The reasons for this were; I loved the gentle nature of the Golden Retriever, but didn't want to deal with the amount of shedding and the breed would be too large for me as a first time dog owner with a fairly small garden. With the Poodle & the Golden Retriever both originally being bred as water dogs, they're lively and up for an adventure. So in short, the miniature goldendoodle was the perfect; size, temperament, low shedding and easily trainable due to the high intelligence of its parent breeds, making it the best breed for us as first time dog owners.

We were renting and viewing houses to buy, as soon as we found the one and started progressing with the paperwork, it's safe to say I had already seen searching online daily for my companion. I came across a post online for F1 (this means the mum and dad are both pedigrees) Miniature Goldendoodle puppies, there were 4 in the litter. I knew that I wanted a cream/golden coloured one as I wanted it to look as close to a golden reteriver as possible, and my heart breaks when I see their little dark features (eyes & nose) amongst the light coloured fluff. I also knew I wanted a girl, as although I knew she would have a season, I couldn't deal with the thought of leg cocking and the boisterous side that comes with having a male dog.

I knew she was the one when I first saw the photo of her on the advert. I reached out to the breeder and paid the deposit as quickly as I could to secure her as ours! It was the longest 6 weeks of my life, waiting for her to be ready to pick up, but moving into our first home during that time helped to keep me busy. We had been living in our house just over 3 weeks when we travelled up to Derbyshire to collect her!

In my 30 years on this planet so far, that was certainly one of the best days I've ever had. The breeder picked her up and put her in my arms, I was shaking like a leaf with excitement, nervousness and every other emotion! Will I be able to give her the best possible life, make sure she is healthy and has everything she needs?

I can safely say that having her in our lives has brought so much joy. She really is that companion I always hoped to have, and being in a lockdown due to a global pandemic certainly reinforced how much I needed the company.

It just so happened that during this time we were living in the pandemic. I had been working from home for almost a year and hadn't seen many family and friends for a long time. My partner was working on the front line daily, which was scary for us with all the uncertainty we were feeling as it was. Having our little fluffy girl helped to brighten up the darker days, and it also meant I had the time to be able to work on her training, working from home I was able to be with her all the time.

I was worried that with her being a lockdown puppy she would get used to always being by my side, and would start to develop separation anxiety if I left her on her own in the future, or would be fearful when someone new entered the house as she had not yet experienced this. But we slowly worked on getting her used to these things once we were able to, and she has now grown to become the most confident, happy go lucky pooch in the world. I feel so lucky to have her, and cannot imagine my life without her in it.
